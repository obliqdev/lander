Revenue Reports

Last updated April 2026 · 5 min read

What's in the report

The Revenue Report gives you a complete financial summary of your studio's income over any date range. It breaks revenue down by source, shows trends over time, and can be exported to CSV for use in your accounting software.

Access note: Revenue Reports are available to Owners and Managers only. Front Desk staff and Instructors cannot access financial reports.

Setting a date range

Navigate to Reporting → Revenue.

  1. Click the Date Range picker at the top of the report. Choose from preset ranges (Today, This Week, This Month, Last Month, Last 30 Days, Last 90 Days, This Year) or set a Custom Range using the calendar picker.

  2. Click Apply. The report updates immediately to show all revenue within the selected period.

  3. Use the Compare To toggle to show a side-by-side comparison with the previous equivalent period (e.g., this month vs. last month). Percentage changes are highlighted in green (increase) or red (decrease).

Understanding the breakdown

Revenue is categorized by source to help you understand which parts of your business are performing:

  • Memberships — recurring billing revenue from active membership plans, net of any paused periods
  • Passes — one-time purchase revenue from pass packs and intro offers
  • Drop-ins — single class or appointment bookings paid individually
  • Appointments — private session revenue not covered by a pass or membership
  • Refunds issued — negative line showing the total refunded in the period (this is deducted from gross revenue to show net)
Tip: A high Memberships-to-Drop-ins ratio is a good sign — it indicates strong retention and predictable monthly revenue. A high Drop-ins figure may suggest clients are not converting to memberships.

Exporting to CSV

  1. With your desired date range selected, click the Export button in the top-right corner of the report.

  2. Choose Export Summary for a single-row summary per revenue category, or Export Transactions for a line-by-line breakdown of every individual transaction in the period.

  3. The CSV downloads immediately. Column headers include: Date, Client Name, Revenue Type, Description, Gross Amount, Fees, Net Amount, Payment Status.

Accounting software: The transaction export is formatted for direct import into Xero, QuickBooks, and Wave. For Xero, use the "Date" column as the transaction date and "Net Amount" as the amount.

Key metrics explained

The top of the report displays four headline metrics for the selected period:

  • Gross Revenue — total charged to clients before refunds and processing fees
  • Net Revenue — gross revenue minus all refunds issued in the period
  • Processing Fees — the total payment processing fees deducted. This is informational only — processing fees are deducted before payout and are not reported to clients
  • Expected Payout — the estimated amount you will receive in your next payout, based on settled transactions minus fees. This may differ slightly from your actual payout due to timing of settlements
