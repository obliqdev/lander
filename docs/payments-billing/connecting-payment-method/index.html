Connecting Your Payment Method

Last updated April 2026 · 6 min read

Before you begin

Obliq processes payments through its integrated payment provider. To receive payouts you need a bank account in the same country as your studio. Owner-level access is required to configure payment settings.

Required documents: Have your business registration number, bank account details (routing and account number), and the account owner's government-issued ID available before you begin. Verification typically takes 1–2 business days.

Step 1 — Open payment settings

Navigate to Settings → Payments.

  1. If no payment provider is connected, you will see a Connect Payments banner. Click it to start the setup flow.

  2. Select your Business Type: Sole Trader, Limited Company, or Non-Profit. This determines what verification documents are required.

  3. Enter your Business Legal Name and Registration Number. For sole traders, enter your full legal name as it appears on your government-issued ID.

Step 2 — Connect your bank account

  1. Enter your bank's Routing Number (or Sort Code in the UK). Double-check this — an incorrect routing number will delay your first payout by 5–7 business days while the mismatch is resolved.

  2. Enter your Account Number. Obliq uses micro-deposit verification for new bank accounts — two small deposits (each under $1) will appear in your bank account within 1–2 business days.

  3. Once the micro-deposits appear, return to Settings → Payments → Verify Account and enter the exact amounts to complete verification.

Important: Do not attempt to process live payments until bank verification is complete. Test mode remains active until verification is confirmed, and test transactions do not result in real charges.

Step 3 — Configure payout schedule

  1. Choose a Payout Frequency: Daily, Weekly, or Monthly. Weekly payouts (every Monday) are the most common choice for studios.

  2. Set the Minimum Payout Amount. Payouts below this amount are held over to the next cycle. Setting this to $0 ensures you receive every dollar promptly.

  3. Review the Processing Fee displayed on this page. Obliq's payment processing fee is deducted before payout — this line shows what percentage is retained per transaction.

Step 4 — Use test mode

Test mode lets you simulate transactions without charging real cards. It is enabled by default until bank verification is complete, but you can also toggle it manually.

  1. In Settings → Payments, toggle Test Mode on. A yellow banner will appear at the top of all payment-related screens to remind you that you are in test mode.

  2. Use the test card number 4242 4242 4242 4242 with any future expiry date and any 3-digit CVC to simulate a successful payment.

  3. Use 4000 0000 0000 0002 to simulate a declined card — useful for testing your failed payment notification emails.

Step 5 — Process a test transaction

  1. Create a test client account in Obliq with a name like "Test Client" — this client will not appear in public listings.

  2. Make a test booking or membership purchase using the test card number. Confirm that the booking confirmation email is received and that the transaction appears in Payments → Transactions.

  3. Check the Payout Preview section to confirm the expected payout amount is correct after fees.

Tip: Run a test of each payment flow (single class, pass purchase, membership signup) before going live to make sure confirmation emails and receipts look correct.

Going live

Once bank verification is complete, toggle Test Mode off in Settings → Payments. Your studio is now ready to accept real payments. The yellow test mode banner will disappear and clients can purchase using their own cards.
