Enabling Notifications

Last updated April 2026 · 4 min read

Obliq can send push notifications to alert you about new bookings, cancellations, waitlist movements, and upcoming classes. Notifications work on iPhone, Android, and desktop browsers — but each platform requires you to grant permission first.

Before you start: For the best notification experience on iPhone, add Obliq to your homescreen first. Push notifications on iOS are only available when the app is installed as a homescreen app.

iPhone & iPad

Push notifications on iPhone require iOS 16.4 or later, and Obliq must be installed as a homescreen app via Safari. Once installed, enabling notifications takes just a few taps.

  1. Log in to Obliq from your homescreen app. Notifications will not work if you access Obliq directly through Safari — they only function when Obliq is installed as a standalone app.

  2. Open your profile menu by tapping your avatar in the top-right corner, then select Notifications.

  3. Grant permission when Obliq prompts you. Tap Allow to let Obliq send you push notifications on this device.

  4. Customize your notification preferences by enabling the event types you want to be alerted about. Toggle on notifications for new bookings, booking updates, waitlist changes, and any other events that matter to your workflow.

  5. You're all set! You'll now receive real-time notifications on your homescreen. When a new booking comes in, you'll see an alert immediately, even when you're not actively using the app.

Tip: For the best experience, enable Sounds in your notification settings. This ensures you'll hear an alert whenever important events happen, even when your phone is face-down or in another room.

Android

Notifications on Android work both in Chrome and when installed as a homescreen app. Chrome will typically show a permission prompt automatically.

  1. Open Obliq in Chrome or from your homescreen if already installed.

  2. When the browser asks "Allow Obliq to send notifications?", tap Allow.

  3. If you dismissed the prompt, tap the lock icon in the Chrome address bar → Notifications → Allow.

  4. For installed homescreen apps, go to Settings → Apps → Obliq → Notifications and ensure notifications are enabled.

Tip: On Android, you can assign Obliq notifications to a priority channel so they appear above other apps in your notification shade. Look for Notification categories in the app's notification settings.

Desktop browsers

Chrome, Edge, and Firefox on Mac and Windows all support web push notifications from Obliq.

  1. Navigate to app.withobliq.com in your browser and log in.

  2. When Obliq asks for notification permission, click Allow in the browser prompt that appears near the address bar.

  3. If you previously clicked "Block," click the lock icon in the address bar, find Notifications, and change it to Allow, then reload the page.

Notifications will appear as system alerts even when the browser is in the background, as long as the browser is open.

Safari on Mac: Push notifications require macOS 13 Ventura or later and Safari 16 or later. The permission flow is the same — click Allow when prompted.

What you'll be notified about

Once enabled, Obliq can send the following types of notifications depending on your role and settings configured in the Notifications menu:

Bookings

  • New booking — A client books a class, appointment, or event. Perfect for instantly knowing when someone signs up.
  • Booking updated — A booking is modified (e.g., a client drops out). Stay notified when attendance changes.
  • Promoted from waitlist — A client moves off the waitlist into an available spot. Great for managing fills on popular classes.
  • Waitlist position changed — A client's position on the waitlist shifts. Helps them know where they stand.
  • Booking cancelled — A client or admin cancels a booking. Admins are alerted to cancellations.

Orders & Passes

  • Order charged — A client is successfully charged for a purchase. Admin notifications for payment receipts.
  • Class pass added — A class pass is added to a client's account. Admins get notified of pass assignments.

Memberships

  • Membership added — A membership is activated for a client. Admins are alerted to new member signups.

Clients & Messages

  • New client signed up — A new client creates an account. Welcome new studio members as they join.
  • New message — A client sends you a direct message. Stay responsive with instant message alerts.

Schedule & Substitutions

  • Sub request created — A substitute request is opened for a class. Instructors can claim subs immediately.
  • Sub request claimed — An instructor has claimed an open sub request. Confirms coverage is secured.

Tip: Not all notification types are enabled by default. Visit the Notifications settings to choose exactly which events matter most to your workflow. You can always adjust these preferences at any time.

Troubleshooting

I'm not receiving notifications on iPhone. Confirm that Obliq is opened from the homescreen app, not from the Safari browser directly. Check Settings → Obliq → Notifications and ensure Allow Notifications is enabled. Also check that Do Not Disturb or Focus mode isn't silencing alerts.

I accidentally blocked notifications in my browser. Click the lock icon in the browser address bar, set Notifications to Allow, and reload the page. Obliq will re-register for notifications automatically.

Notifications work on mobile but not on my desktop. Make sure your browser is not in a private or incognito window — notifications are blocked in private browsing mode. Also check your operating system's Do Not Disturb settings.

I keep getting too many notifications. Go to Settings → Notifications → Staff Alerts in Obliq and disable the event types you don't need.
