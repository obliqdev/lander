Setting Up Appointments

Last updated April 2026 · 5 min read

Overview

Appointments in Obliq handle private, one-on-one sessions between a client and a single instructor. Unlike classes, appointments are booked against a specific instructor's availability window rather than a shared schedule slot.

Before you begin: You need at least one instructor with their availability configured before clients can book appointments.

Step 1 — Create a service type

Navigate to Appointments → Services → New Service.

  1. Enter a Service Name (e.g., "Private Reformer Session" or "Introductory Assessment"). This appears on your booking page and in client confirmation emails.

  2. Add an optional Description — what the session covers, who it is suitable for, and what clients should bring or prepare.

  3. Select which Instructors offer this service. Only instructors with this service selected will appear as available when a client books.

Step 2 — Duration & pricing

  1. Set the Session Duration in minutes. Common values are 30, 45, 60, or 90 minutes. This controls the slot length shown to clients when they pick a time.

  2. Add a Buffer Time after the session (0–30 minutes) to give instructors time to clean equipment, make notes, or prepare for the next client. Buffer time is blocked in the instructor's calendar but not shown to clients.

  3. Set the Price for a single session, or leave it unpiced if this service is covered by memberships or passes.

Tip: Add 10–15 minutes of buffer time after hands-on sessions. It avoids back-to-back pressure and reduces late-running bookings.

Step 3 — Staff availability windows

Clients can only book into times when the instructor is available. Navigate to Staff → [Instructor Name] → Availability.

  1. Set the instructor's Weekly Availability — the days and time ranges when they accept bookings. Each day can have one or more time blocks.

  2. Add any Blocked Dates for holidays, vacations, or other closures. Clients will not see blocked dates as available.

  3. Configure Max Appointments Per Day to prevent over-booking. Once reached, no further bookings are accepted for that day regardless of available time slots.

Step 4 — Booking rules & lead time

  1. Set the Minimum Lead Time — how far in advance a client must book. For example, setting 24 hours means clients cannot book a session starting within the next 24 hours.

  2. Set the Cancellation Window — the minimum notice required for a client to cancel without a fee. Cancellations inside this window may trigger a credit deduction or no-show charge depending on your policy.

  3. Enable Require Intake Form if you want new clients to complete a health questionnaire before their first appointment. The form is sent via email after booking.

Important: Lead time and cancellation window settings are per-service. Set them carefully — once a client has booked, the policy that was in effect at booking time is enforced, even if you change the settings later.

Step 5 — Publish appointments

  1. On the service detail page, toggle the service status to Active. Active services are bookable by clients on your public booking page.

  2. Visit your public booking page and select "Appointments" to confirm the service appears and slots are shown correctly.
